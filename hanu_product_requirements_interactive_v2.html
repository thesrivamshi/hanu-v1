Hanu Product Requirements

Hanu is a self-evolving personal memory, reminder, accountability, relationship, and execution assistant. This document defines what Hanu should do from a product and user-requirement point of view.

Important: This HTML is not a UI design direction and does not prescribe dashboard visuals, layout style, branding, analytics design, or component design. It only documents product features, behavior, user flows, and requirement logic.

Part 1 — Goal-Oriented Hanu

The first product focus is the individual user's goals, reminders, memory, daily execution, follow-up, promises, and relationships. Hanu should convert intention into action and prevent important things from disappearing.

Tell Hanu what matters. Hanu remembers it, organizes it, reminds you, follows up, and helps you close the loop.

Memory

Remembers preferences, goals, people, routines, decisions, promises, and context.

Action

Turns conversations into goals, reminders, open loops, follow-ups, and reviews.

Accountability

Asks for confirmation, tracks misses, detects slips, and guides recovery.

Conversational Assistant

The user should be able to speak naturally. Hanu should understand commands, thoughts, commitments, family responsibilities, and raw dumps.

  • Natural chat for reminders, goals, plans, decisions, reflections, and updates.
  • Voice-note understanding for quick capture while walking, driving, or thinking.
  • Context-aware response based on current goals, time, commitments, relationships, and recent behavior.
  • Quick capture for messy thought dumps.
  • Clarifies whether something is a note, task, reminder, goal, promise, memory, or open loop.
Example: “Dump: check Razorpay, call Aman, think about pricing.” Hanu should extract possible open loops and ask what should become tasks, reminders, or decisions.

Personal Memory

Hanu’s memory must be useful, explainable, correctable, and controlled by the user.

PreferenceGoalRoutinePersonProjectDecisionPromiseBoundaryImportant Date

Memory actions

  • Save on explicit request.
  • Suggest saving important information.
  • Update conflicting memories with confirmation.
  • Forget or delete on request.
  • Show why Hanu knows something.

Memory safety

  • Do not save secrets like passwords or PINs.
  • Ask before saving sensitive details.
  • Allow user to edit, archive, pin, or remove memory.
  • Review stale or duplicate memories over time.

Goals, Execution & Accountability

Hanu should help users create, break down, act on, review, and recover goals.

Goal creation

Capture why it matters, deadline, daily action, minimum action, check-ins, and failure plan.

Commitment strength

Idea, maybe, planned, committed, non-negotiable, promise to someone, family-critical.

Recovery

After misses, ask why, reduce the action, reschedule honestly, or restart with a minimum action.

Failure reason tracking

ForgotToo tiredAvoidedOverplannedNo timeBlocked by someoneWrong timeToo bigNot important anymore
Requirement: Hanu should not only mark failure. It should learn the reason and improve the future plan, reminder time, or task size.

Reminders, Scheduling & Follow-Up

Hanu reminders should not be fire-and-forget. Important reminders should ask for confirmation and follow up until closure or reschedule.

  • One-time reminders.
  • Recurring reminders.
  • Smart reminders suggested from conversation.
  • Confirmation-based reminders.
  • Family reminders.
  • Goal-linked reminders.
  • Waiting-on-someone follow-ups.
  • Review reminders.
1
Reminder sent.
Hanu asks the user to act or confirm.
2
No response.
Hanu follows up based on priority and user’s chosen strictness.
3
Still no response.
Hanu asks for reason, marks as missed, or escalates if permission exists.
4
Recovery.
Hanu suggests the smallest action, honest reschedule, or plan change.

Family, Shared Spaces & Identity Model

This is a core Hanu concept: a family member is not merely stored inside one user’s account. Every person can have their own private Hanu, and shared spaces connect people with explicit permissions.

Person Profile → belongs to Spaces → has Relationships → permissions control access.

Individual profile

Each person can have their own private Hanu with private memories, reminders, goals, preferences, and conversations.

Shared space

A shared area like Family Space, Trusted Circle, Project Space, Care Space, or Education Space. It contains shared responsibilities and allowed context.

Relationship Permissions & Access Rules

People should be able to interact with Hanu around the user, but only within explicit boundaries. Hanu should protect private memory by default.

Question handling rules

Safe shared info

Answer if the requester has permission and the information is shared.

Needs approval

Ask the user before sharing personal or ambiguous information.

Private info

Refuse to share private memories, journals, sensitive categories, or restricted details.

Action request

Forward the request to the user instead of revealing information.

Limited answer

Share a safe summary without exposing private context.

Unknown

Say Hanu does not know or ask the user for confirmation.

Example: Mother asks “Is Vamshi busy?” Allowed answer could be: “He appears unavailable right now. I can ask him to call you.” It should not reveal private work, mood, journal, or failure details.

Requests, Approvals, Relay & Shared Responsibility

Hanu should act as a trusted relay between people, not as a privacy leak. Requests should flow through approvals where needed.

Approval queue

  • Information requests.
  • Reminder requests.
  • Family task requests.
  • Access requests.
  • Shared memory requests.

Message relay

  • “Tell Vamshi to call me.”
  • “Tell Vamshi I uploaded the videos.”
  • “Ask Vamshi if he is free tomorrow.”
  • “Ask him to approve this shared reminder.”

Conflict handling

Requirement: If two people provide conflicting updates, Hanu should not blindly overwrite. It should flag the conflict and ask the right person to confirm.
Example: Mother marks father’s tablet as done. Father says he forgot. Hanu should show a conflicting update and request confirmation.

Promises, Decisions & Relationship Maintenance

Hanu should remember not only tasks, but commitments and decisions that explain the user’s life direction.

Promise tracking

Track what was promised, to whom, by when, current status, and whether it was kept or broken.

Decision log

Remember decisions, why they were made, when they were made, and when to revisit.

Relationship maintenance

Notice neglected people, pending replies, follow-ups, birthdays, and family responsibilities.

Examples: “I promised Aman I’ll send the file.” “We decided not to call Hanu an MVP.” “I decided course is near-term cashflow.”

Memory Inbox, Open-Loop Inbox & Organization Flow

Not everything should become a permanent memory or a scheduled reminder immediately. Hanu needs holding areas where the user can approve, edit, or ignore captured items.

Memory Inbox

Possible memories Hanu found from conversation: preferences, routines, people details, project context, family responsibility.

Open-Loop Inbox

Unfinished items detected from messy thought dumps or conversations.

Inbox-to-organization

User approves categories; Hanu creates goals, reminders, memories, open loops, decisions, or promises.

1
User dumps thoughts.
No structure required.
2
Hanu extracts items.
Possible tasks, memories, decisions, promises, reminders.
3
User approves.
Save, edit, ignore, schedule, or convert.
4
Hanu follows through.
The right item appears in the right product area.

Modes, Boundaries & Notification Behavior

Hanu should adjust behavior based on user state, relationship, time, and importance.

NormalStrictGentleFamilyDeep WorkRecoveryNow ModeLow EnergyQuiet HoursVacation / Pause

When the user asks “What should I do now?”, Hanu should give one clear next action based on current time, energy, top goal, urgency, overdue items, and known avoidance patterns.

Example: “Open the Hanu repo. Work for 25 minutes. Ignore everything else.”
  • Do not message certain people after selected hours.
  • Only urgent reminders during quiet hours.
  • Use different tone by person: strict for user, gentle for parent, professional for friend.

Hanu can support important reminders and escalation, but should not represent itself as emergency medical, legal, or safety support. For true emergencies, the product should direct people to local emergency services or appropriate professionals.

Control Center Requirements, Not Dashboard Design

This section defines what information and controls the product must provide. It does not define the visual dashboard design, layout, style, analytics page, charting, or UI direction.

Do not treat this as visual design instruction. These are product capabilities that must exist somewhere in the experience.

Today Command

Show what matters now: non-negotiable, time-sensitive, family-critical, pending confirmation, open loops.

People & Access

Show people, relationship, spaces, and what they can ask, see, send, or manage.

Approvals

Review incoming information requests, access requests, shared tasks, and relay messages.

Memory Control

Search, edit, delete, correct, pin, archive, and review what Hanu knows.

Activity History

Show what Hanu did, why it did it, who requested it, who saw it, and what was shared.

Life Areas

Organize by self, family, work, money, fitness, health routines, learning, spiritual, content, projects, relationships, home.
